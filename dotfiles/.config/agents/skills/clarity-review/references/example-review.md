# Worked example

The canonical "less clear" email from the Extreme Clarity training, and the kind of review to produce. Read this when you want a concrete model of depth and tone before writing a review.

**Input:**
> Hi everyone, We've heard the feedback from everyone, carefully considered it, and are
> announcing the new LED process today. As far as the process goes, we're standardizing for
> maximum consistency: By Tuesday afternoon, you should sign up for a short review slot then
> send pre-reads by Wednesday end of day. The review is still at the same time as always.
> Finally, don't forget to post notes after the review.

**A good review flags, at minimum:**

- "LED process" — **Context Aware.** First use of the acronym isn't expanded; readers outside
  the originating team don't know it means Launches, Experiments, Decisions. *Fix:* "LED
  (Launches, Experiments, Decisions) reviews".
- "Tuesday afternoon" / "Wednesday end of day" / "same time as always" — **Consistent + Clear.**
  Three different, vague time formats; "same time as always" assumes the reader already knows.
  *Fix:* one consistent format with timezone — "Sign up by Tue 5pm PT", "Send pre-read by Wed
  5pm PT", "Review: Thu 1pm PT".
- "a short review slot" / "post notes" — **Concrete + Clear.** How short? Where do you sign up?
  Where do notes go? *Fix:* "Sign up for a 30-min slot [here](link)", "Post notes in [the team
  Workplace group](link)".
- "As far as the process goes... maximum consistency... don't hesitate to reach out... thank you
  everyone for all the detailed feedback" — **Concise.** Throat-clearing and filler. *Fix:* cut to
  the steps; a single warm closing line is plenty.
- Best practice: the steps are easier to follow as a numbered list / small table (Step → Due by)
  than as prose.
